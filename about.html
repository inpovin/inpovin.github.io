about
I'm Anna - a junior at Vanderbilt University studying Economics and Computer Science. Some of my academic interests include human/computer interaction, behavioral economics, and ethics. I'm also passionate about design, politics, education, and the social impact of technology. I love examining the liminal spaces where all of these subjects intersect - for example, how can we use technology or enact policies to create a more just and beautiful world for all?

At Vanderbilt, I'm a resident of the Mctyeire International Hall, where I dine and discuss international politics/foreign affairs with graduate students, faculty, and my fellow peers. I'm also heavily involved with VandyHacks, a student org dedicated to creating a culture of innovation at Vanderbilt through coding workshops and events. I'm proud to be a part of an org that introduces computer science to students who may not have a background or previous experience in the subject, because I believe that technology should be for all. In my free time, I like to run, read voraciously (everything from news to books to manga), and draw.

I hope to continue growing academically and professionally with a focus on product management in the design or tech-for-good space.
